Linda Altern

Traditional Artist and Graphic Designer

I love creating things, coffee and beach walks with my dog

Love to learn new things

As an artist, my work is a reflection of my personal experiences, beliefs, and the world around me. I am drawn to exploring the complexities of human emotions and the intricate relationships we have with ourselves and others. Through my art, I seek to create a dialogue with the viewer and invite them to engage in their own self-reflection. I use a range of mediums, including painting, drawing, and mixed media, to convey my ideas and emotions. My process often involves starting with an initial concept or feeling and allowing the work to evolve organically as I create. I find inspiration in the natural world, literature, and my own personal journey. In my paintings, I often use bold colors and expressive brushstrokes to convey a sense of energy and movement. I also incorporate layers and textures to create depth and complexity. In my drawings, I use fine lines and delicate shading to capture the subtleties of human expression. One recurring theme in my work is the concept of identity and how we define ourselves. I am fascinated by the idea of the masks we wear and the roles we play in our daily lives. Through my art, I aim to challenge the viewer to consider their own identity and the ways in which they present themselves to the world. Ultimately, my goal as an artist is to create work that is both visually striking and emotionally evocative. I hope to inspire others to embrace their own creativity and to engage with the world in a more introspective and empathetic way.
